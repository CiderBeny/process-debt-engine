📐 Methodology & Sources

1. Annual OPEX Waste

Formula: (Manual hrs/yr + Chase hrs/yr) × Blended Rate × Team Size

Assumptions: 1,800 productive hours per engineer per year — derived from U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics American Time Use Survey (2024), which reports full-time employees average 8.1 hours on workdays, and OECD data (2024) calculating ~1,811 actual hours worked per US worker annually after accounting for paid leave, holidays, and sick time.

Source: BLS American Time Use Survey 2024 (bls.gov); OECD Average Annual Hours Actually Worked 2024 (oecd.org).

2. Risk Exposure

Formula: Annual failures (Q5 × 4) × MTTR (Q11) × Downtime cost/hr × (Risk level / 5)

Assumptions: MTTR (Mean Time To Repair) defaults to 4 hours per incident — a conservative estimate aligned with DORA's "Medium" performer recovery time. Adjust this to match your organisation's actual incident resolution data.

Source: Standard actuarial risk exposure formula (probability × impact) consistent with COSO Enterprise Risk Management framework (2017) and NIST Special Publication 800-30 Rev. 1 — Guide for Conducting Risk Assessments.

3. Pipeline Margin Erosion

Formula: Opportunity margin × 0.25

0.25 multiplier (tool estimate): Reflects the portion of pipeline project margin conservatively estimated as eroded during the first year of delay due to competitive response, customer churn, or SLA penalties. No single authoritative study isolates this fraction; 0.25 is a conservative floor.

Source: Exepron — "The True Cost of Delay" (2026). exepron.com.

4. OPEX-adjusted Estimate

Formula: OPEX Waste × 0.15

0.15 multiplier (tool estimate): A conservative multiplier (0.15) applied to OPEX Waste to account for hidden downstream costs — rework, context switching, and cascading delays that propagate through dependent workflows. This replaces the earlier "Cascade Impact" approach which double-counted OPEX at 0.5×.

Source: Exepron — "The True Cost of Delay" (2026). exepron.com.

6. Turnover Cost Model

Formula: Turnover rate × Team Size × Blended Rate (Q6) × 1,800 hrs

Assumptions: The 50-engineer reference and $150/hr rate are organisational defaults aligned with SHRM's total replacement cost framework. Adjust these to match your actual headcount and cost structures.

Source: SHRM Foundation — "Retaining Talent: A Guide to Analyzing and Managing Employee Turnover" (2025 update). SHRM 2025 Benchmarking Reports, shrm.org/benchmarking.

7. DORA Benchmark Thresholds

The four software delivery performance clusters (Elite, High, Medium, Low) correspond to the 2024 Accelerate State of DevOps Report published by Google Cloud's DORA team. Lead time thresholds: Elite <1 day, High 1 day–1 week, Medium 1 week–1 month, Low >1 month. Deployment frequency: Elite On-demand, High daily–weekly, Medium weekly–monthly, Low monthly–biannually. Note: DORA's four official metrics are Deployment Frequency, Lead Time for Changes, Change Failure Rate, and Failed Deployment Recovery Time. The manual-effort and human-error mappings in this tool are an adaptation, not official DORA categories.

Source: Google Cloud DORA — Accelerate State of DevOps Report 2024. dora.dev/research/2024/dora-report/

9. Total Debt Impact

Formula: OPEX Waste + Risk Exposure + Pipeline Margin Erosion + OPEX-adjusted Estimate

Note: Pipeline Margin Erosion (cOppDirect) is a one-time estimate reflecting margin lost in the first year of delay. All other components recur annually. The OPEX-adjusted Estimate is proportional to OPEX Waste — reducing manual waste automatically reduces this component.

Source: Economic model synthesised from BLS ATUS (2024), OECD Annual Hours (2024), Exepron "True Cost of Delay" (2026), COSO ERM (2017), and SHRM Retention Framework (2025).

10. NPV & IRR Economic Model

Formula: NPV = One-Time Costs + PVIFA × Annual Recurring | IRR = rate where Σ(CFₜ/(1+r)^(t/12)) = 0

Note: One-time costs = Pipeline Margin Erosion + CAPEX. Annual recurring = OPEX Waste + Risk + Cascade. PVIFA discounts the recurring stream using a 10% WACC over a 5-year horizon. Discounted Payback uses monthly DCF to find the break-even month. IRR (Internal Rate of Return) is computed via binary search over monthly cash flows — the rate at which NPV = 0. IRR > WACC (10%) indicates value creation.

Source: Brealey, Myers & Allen — Principles of Corporate Finance. COSO ERM 2017. Damodaran — Cost of Capital by Sector (2025).

11. Configurable Advanced Parameters

Six parameters in the "Advanced Parameters" panel let you override default coefficients in real time: OPEX-adjusted Estimate (0.0–0.3), Pipeline Erosion Rate (0.0–1.0), WACC / Discount Rate (5–20%), NPV Time Horizon (3–10 years), Automation Lever Recovery (10–60%), and Risk Lever Recovery (20–80%). Changing any slider immediately recalculates all dependent metrics. Defaults are restored on page reload.

Source: User-configurable — overrides the Economic Model defaults (Section 10) in real time.
